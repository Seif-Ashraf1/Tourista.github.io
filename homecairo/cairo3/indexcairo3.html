CAIRO TOWER
Its height reaches 187 meters, which is about 43 meters higher than the Great Pyramid of Giza. At the top of the Cairo Tower, there is a tourist restaurant on a revolving platform that takes restaurant patrons to see the landmarks of Cairo from all sides. The tower is considered one of the most prominent landmarks of Cairo. It consists of 16 floors and stands on a base of Aswan granite stones that were previously used by the ancient Egyptians in building their temples and tombs. The trip inside the tower’s elevator to reach its end takes 45 seconds.

AHL MASR WALK BRIDGE
Developing the Nile Corniche walkway from May 15 Bridge to Imbaba Bridge. The length of the project is about 1.8 square kilometers. 90.4% of the project work, which was launched on September 1, 2019, has been completed. The project work is scheduled to be completed by February 17, 2021. The estimated value of the project is 585 million pounds. 19 buildings are being implemented in the project area, including 5 restaurants, 5 cafeterias, and 62 shops.

THE NILE RIVER
Over time, Al-Qahra has been the center of historical monuments, playing a cultural role and having many tourist attractions and landmarks. The Nile is divided north of Cairo, 23 km away, into two branches that feed the Mediterranean Sea: the Rosetta Branch, which heads to the west and is 239 km long, and the Damietta Branch, which heads to the east, and is 245 km long.
