The Blue Hole
ne of Dahab’s most famous sites, the Blue Hole is considered the world’s deadliest diving spot. But its infamous reputation has only boosted the impressive sinkhole’s appeal to brave divers looking for an underwater Everest. Descending to a maximum depth of 328 feet and easily accessible from the shore, the Blue Hole, is shielded from the currents, so its waters are relatively calm. The conditions here are perfect for free divers keen to learn, as well as advanced divers looking to push their limits in a race against depth and lung capacity. While it’s entirely possible to scuba dive safely in the Blue Hole, its notoriety is due to an infamous archway known as the Arch, which connects the site with the open sea.
