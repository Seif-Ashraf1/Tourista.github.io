Hawara pyramid

The Pyramid of Hawara is one of the pyramids of Egypt.[1] It was built by Egyptian Pharaoh Amenemhat III of the 12th Dynasty in the village of Hawara, 9 km southeast of Fayoum. It is made of mud brick covered with limestone. The original height of the pyramid was 58 metres, and the length of each side was 105 metres. Only 20 meters remain of its height now. The pyramid contains many corridors and rooms, ending with the burial chamber. He found a huge stone coffin made of a single piece of quartzite weighing 110 tons. The door to the room was closed with a huge stone, which fell shut by seeping sand underneath into two small side rooms.

Valley of the Whales

Wadi Al-Hitan is located within the Wadi Al-Rayyan Reserve, which covers an area of ​​1,759 square kilometers in Fayoum Governorate, 150 kilometers from Cairo, Egypt. In Wadi Al-Hitan, 10 complete skeletons of whales were found that lived in that area about 40 million years ago, when it was part of a large ocean that included North Africa. In 2005, the Wadi al-Hitan area was classified as a World Heritage Area and was chosen by UNESCO as the best World Heritage area for whale skeletons. Valley of the Whales is famous for the presence of complete whale fossils that filled the area 40 million years ago when the valley was located under a huge ocean (the Teth Sea), in addition to many marine fossils that can be seen in the open museum, which allows visitors to see the whales without touching them to protect them.

Fayoum drivers

Fayoum is famous for the manufacture of waterwheels, which are driven by the power of water and are used to transport water from a lower level to a higher level without the presence of animals or machines. The governorate has more than 200 waterwheels distributed throughout it, the most famous of which are the four waterwheels located in a famous square in Fayoum.The manufacture of waterwheels in Fayoum goes back more than two thousand years, as they were invented in the Ptolemaic era after the ancient Egyptians turned to agriculture in Fayoum, which has a special nature, as it consists of slopes that begin in the south at a height of 26 meters above sea level, and end at a height of 44 meters below the surface.

Ain Al-Silain Gardens

The tourist area of ​​Ain al-Silayin is considered one of the most famous tourist attractions in Fayoum, as it enjoys greenery, green terraces, waterfalls, the old mill, and water springs. It is also distinguished by its hotels, restaurants, and markets for tourist products.
Ain El-Silain is located in a green area with abundant trees in the heart of the Fayoum Delta, where fruit gardens and orchards surround the terraces that fill the place. It is in the middle of the distance between the city of Fayoum, which is located 8 km away from it, and Lake Qarun, which is located 13 km away from it.

Qarun Palace

Qasr Qarun Temple, as mentioned in archaeological sources, is a temple from the Greco-Roman era and was dedicated to the worship of the god Sobek and “Dionysius,” the Roman god of wine and love. However, the inhabitants of the region in Islamic times called it Qasr Qarun because of its presence near Lake Qarun, which is adjacent to it. Which was given this name due to the large number of horns and bays in it, so it was initially called Lake (Al-Qurun), and it was distorted to Lake Qarun. Knowing that this lake was originally the remaining remnant of “Lake Maurice” in Pharaonic history, and as for the number of rooms in the temple, it is less than a hundred rooms, and it was used to store grains and for the uses of the temple priests at this time.

Rayan Valley

Wadi Al-Rayyan Reserve is located in the southwestern part of Fayoum Governorate. Wadi Al-Rayyan consists of the upper lake, the lower lake, the waterfalls area that connects the two lakes, the Oyoun Al-Rayyan area south of the lower lake, the Jabal Al-Rayyan area, which is the area surrounding the eyes, and the Jabal Al-Mudawara area, which is located near The lower lake, and Wadi Al-Rayyan is distinguished by its integrated desert environment, including sand dunes, natural springs, various plant life, various animals, as well as marine fossils. The waterfalls area is also considered one of the areas for various marine sports.
